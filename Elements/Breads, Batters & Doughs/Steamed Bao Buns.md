### Steamed Bao Buns

### Ingredients
#ingredients 
- [ ] 300g (2 1/2 cups) plain flour
- [ ] 50g (1/4 cup) cornflour
- [ ] 10g (2 tsp) instant dried yeast
- [ ] 10g (2 tsp) baking powder
- [ ] 50g (1/4 cup) caster sugar
- [ ] 5g (1 tsp) salt
- [ ] 15ml (1 Tbsp) neutral oil
- [ ] 180ml (3/4 cup) lukewarm water

---
#### Instructions
1.  **Combine Dry Ingredients:** In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the plain flour, cornflour, instant dried yeast, baking powder, caster sugar, and salt. Make sure everything is thoroughly combined to ensure even distribution of the leavening agents.
2.  **Add Wet Ingredients:** Pour the vegetable oil and lukewarm water into the dry ingredients. Use a wooden spoon or your hands to mix until a shaggy dough forms.
3.  **Knead the Dough:** Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Knead for 10-15 minutes until the dough is smooth, elastic, and no longer sticky. It should spring back slowly when poked. This kneading develops the gluten, which is vital for that lovely chewy-soft texture. If you have a stand mixer with a dough hook, you can use that for about 8-10 minutes on medium speed.
4.  **First Prove:** Lightly grease your mixing bowl with a little oil. Place the kneaded dough in the bowl, turning it once to coat. Cover the bowl with a damp tea towel or cling film (plastic wrap) and let it prove in a warm place for 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size. A good warm spot could be an oven with just the light on, or near a sunny window.
5.  **Punch Down & Shape:** Once doubled, gently punch down the dough to release the air. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for another 2-3 minutes to get rid of any large air bubbles. Roll the dough into a long log, about 4-5 cm (1.5-2 inches) in diameter.
6.  **Portion the Buns:** Cut the log into 12-16 equal pieces, depending on your desired bun size. Each piece should weigh roughly 40-50g.
7.  **Form the Bao:** Take one piece of dough and flatten it into a rough oval or round shape, about 8-10 cm (3-4 inches) in diameter. You can use a rolling pin for this, but don't roll it too thin â€“ you want some substance for the fluffiness.
    *   **For folded 'taco' style bao:** Place a small piece of baking paper (parchment paper) under each flattened oval. Fold the dough in half gently, creating a semi-circle. You can place a chopstick lightly across the middle before folding to ensure it doesn't stick together completely. Remove the chopstick after folding.
    *   **For enclosed round bao:** Bring the edges of the dough up and pinch them together at the top, forming a pleat pattern, or simply bring all edges to the centre and pinch to seal. Place seam-side down on a square of baking paper.
8.  **Second Prove:** Arrange the shaped buns on squares of baking paper (this stops them sticking to the steamer). Place them in your steamer basket, ensuring there's plenty of space between each bun as they will expand significantly. Cover the steamer basket with its lid (or a damp tea towel if your lid isn't airtight) and let them prove for another 30-45 minutes, or until they look visibly puffy and light. This second prove is crucial for the final soft texture!
9.  **Steam the Buns:** Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil. Carefully place the steamer basket over the boiling water. Steam the buns for 8-10 minutes. Do not lift the lid during steaming, especially for the first 5-7 minutes, as this can cause the buns to collapse.
10. **Rest and Serve:** Once steamed, turn off the heat but leave the lid on the steamer for another 2-3 minutes. This allows the buns to gradually cool and prevents them from deflating.
11. **Enjoy!** Carefully remove the buns and serve immediately with your favourite fillings.

#### Notes:
Troubleshooting Tips for a Fail-Proof Bao:
*   **Yeast Activation:** Ensure your water is lukewarm, not hot. Water that's too hot will kill the yeast, and too cold won't activate it. If you're unsure, you can "proof" your yeast first: dissolve it in the lukewarm water with a pinch of sugar for 5-10 minutes. If it gets foamy, your yeast is active!
*   **Kneading is Key:** Don't skimp on the kneading time. This develops the gluten, which is essential for that characteristic chewy-soft texture. An under-kneaded dough will result in dense, heavy buns.
*   **Proving Environment:** A warm, draught-free place is ideal for proving. If your kitchen is cold, the proving will take longer. You can create a warm spot by turning your oven on its lowest setting for a few minutes, then turning it off and placing the dough inside (with the door ajar if it's still warm).
*   **Don't Over-Flour:** When kneading and shaping, use just enough flour to prevent sticking. Too much added flour will make the buns tough and dry.
*   **Patience with the Second Prove:** This is perhaps the most important step for fluffiness. The buns should look noticeably puffy before steaming. If they're under-proved, they'll be dense.
*   **Steaming Technique:** Don't overcrowd your steamer. Give the buns space to expand. And resist the urge to peek during steaming! The sudden drop in temperature can cause them to deflate.
